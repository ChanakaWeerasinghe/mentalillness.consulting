Meditation as a process - any practice leading to a state of meditative trance. Prayer, dance, concentration … - meditation can be any action, if it ultimately leads to a state of meditative trance.

Historically, meditation (from the Latin. Meditatio - reflection, pondering) is one of the techniques of Indian spiritual practice , therefore everything connected with meditation traditionally has a touch of esotericism, religiosity and something magical. See Traditional forms of meditation for more details .

Meditative Immersion Techniques The literature describes hundreds of different methods for immersion in a state of meditative trance. Studies have shown that the most important (and common to almost all meditative practices) elements of such an immersion are two things: 1) lack of tension and 2) deep concentration on a monotonously repeating action. Such an action can be either physical (for example, ritual dances in African tribes; exercises in martial arts; sorting out beads; reading the mantra aloud; concentration on the breath; etc.), or mental, imaginary (for example, concentration on the chakra; concentration on pulsations of the “astral body”; etc.).

Despite the fact that scientific studies do not confirm the relationship between the depth of meditation and posture, the most recommended posture for meditation is as follows: sitting, back straight, head slightly lowered, legs crossed, hands on the knees with palms up, palms open, thumb and forefinger connected. If a person calms down, relaxes and focuses on the chakra or mantra, this is the meditation of a lover of oriental culture. It is no worse if a person focuses his attention on his breath or on the flame of a candle. And our men at this time go fishing and, until they drink, focus on the float, which stopped in the middle of a quiet backwater. Great meditation!

Use of meditation Meditation is often used in psychotherapeutic and spiritual practices for a wide variety of purposes, for example, to relieve tension, general relaxation, create a feeling of lightness and happiness, to relieve the habit of negative thinking, create a basis for positive attitudes and affirmations, sometimes to easily suggest to the client some or images and beliefs affecting him. It is clear that this gives great scope for creative people - both for effective psychotherapy and psychotherapy for the sick and philosophical sermons of different quality.

Meditation is definitely good for mental and physical health . If you remove the high words and aspirations, then the real mechanism of meditation is simple and has long been known to science: it is desensitization. Back in the early twentieth century, researchers discovered that fears and memories of traumatic situations are easily and effectively removed if the image that worries us is viewed by us against the background of complete bodily relaxation. Actually, if any image or thought becomes anchored in muscle tension, we will get an image that strains us and thoughts that are difficult for us. If, on the contrary, we scroll through problematic thoughts and hard memories against a background of a relaxed body (plus combine with a bright inner background and soft relaxing music), then the tension and heaviness will quickly disappear. And the soul will be - easy!

There is nothing new here that goes beyond the limits of the work of combining reflexes according to Bekhterev - no. Today, all NLP practitioners and almost all psychotherapists use this, not always understanding the real mechanism of the useful procedures that they use.

Meditations are fashionable and sell well, especially when you are promised a “state of complete awareness” along with health benefits. Here, please be careful: a “state of full awareness” is often promised, but in real practice it is more often absent, giving way, on the contrary, to a state of uncritical suggestibility.

Is this good or bad? It depends. If the client does not listen to anyone and suffers from it himself, then any specialist psychotherapist, in order not to suffer from his resistance, will offer him, under one pretext or another, to relax, close his eyes, after which he will most likely arrange a very useful “spiritual journey”, as a result of which the client seems to get to everything that the psychotherapist has easily instilled in him. And really helped. Could this be harmful or dangerous? Theoretically - yes, almost more often it is very useful. A fraudster will deprive you of money without any meditation, problematic suggestions from watching television broadcasts significantly block any possible harm from meditation … Everything is determined by those who meditate with you. If a person is wise and worthy, learn from him and say thanks to him. Do not trust him - look for another teacher or specialist.
