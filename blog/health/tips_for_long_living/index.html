All of these, of course, are the obvious steps that you already knew about. But researchers found that long-livers share their characteristics in nutrition, exercise, ways to deal with stress - all this we can use to slow down our aging process. Of course, the likelihood that you will live to be 100 years old is higher if your ancestors were centenarians. But here the question arises: what if your ancestors did not live their 100 years (although they had a longevity gene) just because they had an unhealthy way of life - they drank, smoked, had a passive way of life, etc., not waiting for “their” age? In short, follow these 10 rules if you want to live a long life …

1. Do not retire.

“Experience shows that when a person who suddenly stops working, obesity and chronic diseases begin to develop,” says Luigi Ferrucci, director of the Baltimore Longitudinal Study of Aging. In Chianti (Italy), which is famous for its centenarians, there is a wide variety of ways for leisure. “After leaving their jobs, the Italians spend most of their time on small farms, cultivating wine and vegetables,” says Luigi Ferrucci. “They never are passive.” Is farming not for you? Settle in the museum! Yes and in general - mass options, even if you will work for little money or even for free.

2. Use floss every day.

This can help keep your arteries healthy. In 2008, a study was tested at the University of New York (New York University), which found that daily use of dental floss reduces the number of pathogenic bacteria in the mouth. These bacteria can enter the blood circulation and cause various inflammatory lesions in the arteries, which is the main risk factor for heart diseases. “I believe that people should use the floss twice a day to get the expected effect,” says Dr. Perls.

3. Get moving!

“Physical activity is the only real source of your youth that exists,” said Jay Olshansky, a professor of medicine, an aging researcher at the University of Illinois, Chicago. “It looks like gasoline and engine oil for your car. You should not do this, but your car will definitely drive better. ” According to numerous studies thanks to exercise, mood, mental acuity, muscle mass and bone are improved.

“The benefits come immediately after your first training session,” adds Olshanski. Do not worry if you do not have a subscription to the fitness center (or you don’t have enough money to buy it). Walk to friends (on foot, of course) or go to a local park. A half day of walks will be enough for a start. If you don’t want to build muscle with the help of trainers, try yoga - you don’t need to lift it.

4. Eat fiber-rich cereals on breakfast.

Consuming whole grains, especially for breakfast, helps keep sugar levels stable throughout the day.

5. Sleep at least six hours a day.

By adding the missing sleep hours, you will add a few years to your life. “Sleep is one of the most important functions that our body uses to regulate and restore cells,” says Dr. Ferrucci. “We calculated that the minimum amount of sleep that a person needs to get the fast eye movement phase (REM phase) is about six hours.”

6. Eat whole grains,

without additives. People whose blood has a high level of certain nutrients - selenium, beta-carotene, vitamin C and E, as a rule live longer and have better cognitive indicators. Unfortunately, there is no evidence that taking medications with these trace elements and vitamins will have a similar effect. There are more than 200 different carotenoids and 200 different flavonoids in a single tomato, “says Ferrucci. There are fewer white foods - bread, flour, sugar and more colored vegetables and fruits, and dark whole grain breads and cereals.

7. Do not be nervous.

According to a new study, centenarians usually do not take problems to heart and live with troubles. In short, they simply do not steam. If this is an innate trait, find a way for yourself that allows you to manage your stress: yoga, exercise, meditation, tai chi, and give deep breathing in the end! -Watch, chips in front of the TV, booze? No, this is a very bad option.

8. Live as Seventh Day Adventists.

Americans who consider themselves Seventh-day Adventists have an average life span of 89 years. Almost 10 years more than the average American lives. One of the main principles of this religion is to take care of your body. This means not to smoke, not to abuse alcohol and sweet. Followers of this religion usually follow a veggie diet based on fruits and vegetables, beans and nuts, and they are also active in physical exercise. And also they pay great attention to the family and society.

9. Habits.

Long-livers usually have a strict order, says Dr. Olshanski. - They adhere to a certain diet and engage in certain physical activities throughout life. They wake up and go to bed at the same time. This is another good habit that keeps your body in equilibrium. “Your physiology becomes fragile as you age,” says Ferrucci. - “And the more difficult it is for your body to bounce back if you, say, miss a few hours of sleep or drink a lot of alcohol.” This can weaken the immune system, making you more susceptible to viral infections.

10. Contact.

Regular contact with your friends and family is the key to avoiding depression, which can lead to early death. Some psychologists believe that the main benefit for older people from physical exercise is social interaction.
